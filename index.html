Overview

Executive Summary

In our project, we address Horizon State University's growing challenge of declining retention during the first two years of undergraduate study. We identified that advisors currently lack a unified and proactive way to monitor academic, financial, and engagement risk factors, because this information is spread across disconnected systems. This fragmentation leads to delays, missed warnings, and inconsistent interventions, ultimately increasing the chances of academic failure or withdrawal. To solve this problem, we developed the Student Risk Monitoring Dashboard, a centralized platform built using artificially generated student data. Our system brings together academic records, attendance, financial aid indicators, and engagement behaviors into one dashboard. By applying predictive analytics, the platform identifies early signs of students at risk of program dropout and provides advisors with real-time alerts and personalized recommendations that they can send to students. This allows advisors to intervene earlier and focus their attention on the students who need support the most.

Research & Insights

Research Foundations

  • Learning Analytics Framework: Applied established learning analytics principles from educational research to structure our data collection and analysis approach, ensuring alignment with proven methods for identifying at-risk student behaviors
  • Dashboard Design Principles: Leveraged research on educational data visualization and cognitive load theory to create an interface that advisors can quickly interpret and act upon without extensive training
  • Early Warning Systems: Reviewed literature on predictive models in higher education, incorporating best practices from institutions that successfully reduced attrition through proactive intervention strategies

Key Learnings & Achievements

  • Multi-Factor Risk Assessment: Discovered that combining academic, engagement, and financial indicators provides significantly more accurate risk prediction than any single factor alone, confirming the importance of holistic student monitoring
  • Engagement as Early Indicator: Research revealed that disengagement signals (low LMS logins, missed advisor meetings, declining tutoring attendance) often precede academic decline by several weeks, creating critical intervention windows
  • Synthetic Data Validity: Successfully created a realistic dataset reflecting national benchmarks for first-generation students, GPA distributions, and attendance patterns, validating that synthetic data can effectively simulate real-world scenarios for model development
  • Advisor-Centric Design: User research with academic advisors identified that alerts, risk thresholds, and one-click email interventions significantly improve workflow efficiency and intervention response times
  • Scalability Validation: Demonstrated that our architecture can handle large-scale datasets (18,000 students) while maintaining responsive performance through strategic caching and data optimization techniques

By consolidating fragmented data from multiple systems into a single predictive analytics platform, the project eliminates delays in identifying struggling students and enables targeted, timely support that improves retention outcomes.

It aligns with institutional goals to reduce first- and second-year attrition, improve graduation rates, and demonstrate data-driven decision-making in student services. Architecture & Data Design

System Architecture

The Student Risk Monitoring Dashboard operates across three integrated layers: Business Architecture, IS Architecture, and Tech Architecture, creating a unified, secure, and data-driven ecosystem that supports early detection and targeted student success interventions.

Enterprise Architecture

Business Architecture

Outlines core student and faculty processes including user authentication, profile access, at-risk student identification, and academic recommendation workflows. Captures engagement and monitoring activities such as attendance tracking, LMS logins, and analytics that inform early alerts and personalized student interventions.

Information Systems Architecture

Shows how student data flows through the system: synthetic CSV files are created and loaded, cached for performance, grouped by risk factors, and processed through predictive models. Includes the Streamlit dashboard for real-time monitoring, visual analytics, downloadable CSV exports, and automated email recommendations for at-risk students.

Technology Architecture

Built on Python runtime with Streamlit as the UI framework, local file storage for data persistence, and integrated security features including salted SHA-256 hashing, credential store, and TLS encryption. Utilizes Plotly for interactive visualizations and Pandas/NumPy for data manipulation.

Entity-Relationship Diagram

Database Schema & Entity Relationships

The comprehensive relational database model consists of eight interconnected entities, each serving a specific function in the student risk monitoring ecosystem:

  • Students (Central Entity): Core table containing student_id (primary key), age, gender, ethnicity, first_generation_flag, major, residence, enrollment_status, and financial_aid_flag. Acts as the hub connecting all other entities through foreign key relationships.
  • Academic_Records: Stores term-by-term academic performance including record_id, student_id (foreign key), term, credits_attempted, course_drop_count, late_registration, gpa_term, cum_gpa, and probation_flag. One-to-many relationship from students (one student has multiple academic records across terms).
  • Engagement_Metrics: Tracks student participation with engagement_id, student_id (foreign key), term, lms_logins, attendance_rate, assignments_on_time_pct, discussion_posts, and library_visits. Captures behavioral indicators that predict retention risk.
  • Support_Services: Records utilization of support resources via service_id, student_id (foreign key), term, advisor_meetings, and tutoring_sessions. Helps identify students not accessing available help.
  • Financial_Records: Manages financial data through financial_id, student_id (foreign key), term, outstanding_balance, and work_hours_per_week. Links financial stress to academic risk.
  • Risk_Assessments: Contains predictive model outputs including assessment_id, student_id (foreign key), term, dropout_probability, and at_risk_flag. Stores ML-generated risk scores for advisor action.
  • Professors: Maintains faculty information with professor_id (primary key), username, email, and password_hash for secure authentication and intervention tracking.
  • Interventions: Logs advisor actions through intervention_id, student_id (foreign key), professor_id (foreign key), intervention_type, intervention_date, and notes. Creates accountability trail for intervention effectiveness analysis.

Key Relationships: The students table serves as the central hub with one-to-many relationships to academic_records, engagement_metrics, support_services, financial_records, risk_assessments, and interventions. The interventions table has a many-to-one relationship with professors, enabling tracking of which advisors perform which interventions. This normalized structure prevents data redundancy while enabling comprehensive student profiles assembled through JOIN operations across all dimensions.

  • Integration Testing: Ensuring predictions merged accurately with original dataset required robust integrity checks to prevent missing or mismatched records.

This project pushed our team to think critically about the intersection of data, technology, and student success. We entered with a straightforward goal to build a risk monitoring dashboard, but quickly realized the complexity of creating a truly useful tool that balances predictive power with ethical responsibility.

Each team member brought unique strengths: some excelled at data engineering, others at machine learning, and some at visualization design and user experience. We learned to leverage these differences and work collaboratively. Our most productive moments came when we embraced diverse perspectives and challenged assumptions about what "at-risk" means and how to intervene effectively.

The most valuable lesson was understanding that technology alone doesn't solve problems, it enables solutions when combined with deep user understanding, ethical considerations, and iterative refinement. We're proud not just of what we built, but of how we built it: collaboratively, thoughtfully, and with student success at the center.

Project Impact & Outcomes

The Student Risk Monitoring Dashboard delivers a fully functional application that demonstrates how predictive analytics and centralized insights can support student success at Horizon State University. By consolidating fragmented data, including GPA, attendance, course completion, engagement metrics, work hours, and financial aid into a single dashboard, advisors can identify at-risk students efficiently and take targeted interventions.

In the long term, the platform has the potential to increase retention, improve academic performance, and reduce program dropout rates, mitigating financial and reputational losses for the university. This project validated the power of research-backed design, ethical data practices, and collaborative development. Most importantly, it showed that when institutions have access to proactive, data-driven insights, they can create an equitable advising culture that supports every student's path to success.
